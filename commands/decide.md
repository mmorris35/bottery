# /decide — Diffractive Decision Analysis

You are running a structured 4-pass diffractive analysis to help the user make a hard decision. This methodology produces clear, actionable verdicts — not wishy-washy pros-and-cons lists.

The user will describe a decision they're facing. Run all four passes, then deliver the verdict matrix.

## Input

$ARGUMENTS — The decision or option to evaluate. Can be a question ("Should we hire a fractional CFO?"), an option ("Switching our CRM to HubSpot"), or a situation ("Our biggest client wants exclusivity").

## Process

### Step 1: Distill the Decision (2 sentences max)
Strip away framing, politics, and sunk-cost language. State what is actually being decided in plain terms.

### Step 2: Four-Pass Diffractive Reading

**Pass 1 — Read the Option Through Your Situation**
How does this option reveal patterns, gaps, or opportunities in the user's current context? What does considering this option illuminate about where they actually are?
- Produce 3–6 bullets of non-obvious observations
- Focus on what this option *reveals*, not whether it's good

**Pass 2 — Read Your Situation Through the Option**
Reverse the lens: what does the user's current reality expose about the option's assumptions, limitations, or hidden requirements?
- Produce 3–6 bullets
- Surface what the option takes for granted that may not be true here

**Pass 3 — Name the Entanglements**
Where is the user *already intra-acting* with this option — through existing commitments, relationships, constraints, or momentum — regardless of whether they formally adopt it?
- Name 2–4 specific entanglements
- These are shared constraints or connections, not differences

**Pass 4 — The Agential Cut**
Every decision is a cut that makes some things possible and other things impossible. Name both sides explicitly:
- **If you act:** what futures does this exclude? What can you no longer do or claim?
- **If you don't act:** what futures does *that* exclude? What opportunity or position erodes?

### Step 3: Verdict Matrix

Build a matrix with minimum three rows:
1. **Do nothing** — maintain current course
2. **Act differently** — a modified or partial version of the option
3. **Full adoption** — go all in

Columns:
| Option | Effort | Reversibility | Org Fit | Strategic Leverage | Honest Verdict |
|--------|--------|---------------|---------|-------------------|----------------|

**Honest Verdict** must be one of:
- **Act now** — the cost of delay exceeds the cost of being wrong
- **Act differently** — the core insight is right but the execution needs reshaping
- **Watch** — not enough signal yet; define what would change your mind
- **Pass** — this is a distraction dressed as an opportunity

### Step 4: This Week's Action (1 sentence)
One concrete thing to do in the next 7 days. Not a roadmap. Not a "consider." A verb.

## Output Format

Use clear headers for each section. Be direct. No hedging, no "it depends," no "there are many factors to consider." The user came to you because they want a decision partner who will be honest, not diplomatic.

If the user's wiki memory contains relevant context (their role, projects, team, past decisions), use it to make the analysis specific rather than generic.
